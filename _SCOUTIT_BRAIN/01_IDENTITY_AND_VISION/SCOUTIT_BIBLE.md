## PART 1 — WHAT SCOUTIT IS (AND IS NOT)

### The One-Line Truth
ScoutIt is a **Spatial Commerce platform** — not a real estate website.

The difference matters. A real estate website lists properties. ScoutIt **treats physical space as a liquid, transactable, data-dense asset** — and builds an entire intelligence ecosystem around it. It does for *space* what Bloomberg did for *finance*: surface the signals that professionals hide, present them in an editorial format, and let serious people make serious decisions.

### The Market Category ScoutIt Invented
**Spatial Commerce** = the programmatic liquidation, utilization, and data-dense mapping of physical space.

It is not "PropTech." It is not "a listing site." It is a new category that covers:
- Residential (condos, houses, lots)
- Commercial (office, retail, warehouse)
- STR / Short-Term Rentals
- Hospitality (hotels, boutique stays)
- Culinary / Restaurants
- Event Venues
- Any structure with four walls and a story

ScoutIt's radical claim: **every kind of space is the same product in disguise.** They all have square meters, location signals, foot traffic patterns, and a buyer/tenant on the other side. Unify the intelligence layer, and you unlock a category nobody else has touched in the Philippines.

## PART 3 — THE VISION

### Why ScoutIt Exists

The Philippine real estate market is dominated by pressure-driven listing sites. You search, you see a price, a broker calls you within 10 minutes, you are sold to. The space itself — its architecture, its history, its neighborhood pulse, its real market position — is almost never surfaced.

ScoutIt's founding belief: **a space is more than a spec sheet. It is where life unfolds.**

The platform was built to fix three things:
1. **No signal** — buyers make decisions on hype, not data
2. **No access** — the best spaces are hidden behind broker gatekeeping
3. **No privacy** — the moment you express interest, you get sold to

ScoutIt responds with:
1. **Intelligence** — architectural DNA, market signals, location narratives, curated editorial
2. **Access** — a unified directory across all space categories, open to browse
3. **Privacy** — the Ledger (wishlist) lives on the user's device, never on a server

### The Manifesto in One Breath

> No guesswork. No gatekeeping. No pressure.
> The signals are real. The spaces are real.
> The platform is yours.

### RA 9646 — Legal Compliance (clarified 2026-06-26)

ScoutIt operates in compliance with the **Philippine Real Estate Service Act (RA 9646)**. Compliance is non-negotiable and is noted in the site footer.

**Key clarification:** Connects is NOT brokerage activity. It is an authorization and anti-spam layer:
- Connects gates WHO can be listed on a property — not the transaction itself
- The handshake = "this broker has the owner's blessing to appear on this listing"
- ScoutIt never facilitates, negotiates, or takes a cut of any transaction
- ScoutIt resolves platform disputes only — never transaction disputes
- This has been reviewed and confirmed: the model is clean under RA 9646

**The ephemeral chat:** messages between broker and owner are deleted immediately when the chat closes. ScoutIt does not retain chat contents. This must appear in the Terms of Service and as a disclaimer inside every chat window.

## PART 4 — THE BUSINESS MODEL (B2B2C)

ScoutIt is a **B2B2C Ecosystem Platform**. It monetizes two distinct audiences simultaneously.

### The B2B Side — The Ecosystem
ScoutIt sells tools, visibility, and verification to the professionals who serve buyers:

| Role | What they pay for |
|---|---|
| **Brokers / Advisors** | Verified profile badge, subscription tiers (visibility ranking, featured listings) |
| **Space Photographers** | Portfolio listing, lead generation from property owners |
| **Site Researchers** | Service listing, inbound inquiries |
| **Event Designers** | Directory placement, client discovery |

Subscription tiers map to **Connects** (the platform currency — see Part 6).

### The B2C Side — The Buyer
ScoutIt serves high-intent buyers, investors, and researchers who want intelligence without pressure:

| Feature | What they get |
|---|---|
| **The Ledger** | Private wishlist, stored on their device — no account required |
| **Property Intelligence** | Editorial briefings: architectural DNA, market signals, location context |
| **Discovery Feed** | Curated spotlights, news, collections — editorial curation, not algorithm slop |
| **Double-Blind Privacy Shield** | Future feature: connect with brokers without revealing identity until ready |

### The Dual-Monetization Architecture (Connects vs. Subscriptions)

ScoutIt strictly separates the **Communication Layer** from the **Deep Intelligence Layer** to maintain trust and UX integrity. We do not block inquiries behind subscriptions.

1. **The Handshake (Connects)**
   - **For Seekers:** Public facts (Price, GLA, Location, Condition) are free. If a seeker wants to inquire, they spend a **Connect** token to message the broker/owner. 
   - **Psychology:** Pay-per-connect ensures high-intent leads without forcing buyers into subscriptions just to talk.

2. **The Vault (Subscriptions for Seekers)**
   - **For Seekers:** Access to "Deep Intelligence" (Luma AI 3D Spatial Maps, 360 AR Tours, Acoustic Heatmaps, Negotiation Buffers, exact Floor Levels). 
   - **Psychology:** This is an "Ocular Replacement Upgrade." Busy executives or overseas investors pay a monthly subscription to skip physical site visits and do extreme due diligence from their desk.

3. **The Deployment Queue (Subscriptions for Owners)**
   - **For Owners:** We do *not* promise instant 3D generation. Subscribing to an upper tier (e.g., Orbit Layer) places the owner in a prioritized queue where ScoutIt deploys QuestIT Pros to scan their property. 
   - **The Fast Lane:** If an owner is impatient, they can use the QuestIT marketplace to pay an expedited bounty, or upload their own videos for our Luma AI to process.

4. **The Churned Owner Escrow (Data Ownership)**
   - **The Dilemma:** What happens if an owner cancels their subscription, but ScoutIt already generated a highly valuable 3D Spatial Map for them?
   - **The Hook:** ScoutIt owns the derivative 3D data. The 3D map stays active in The Vault for Premium Seekers. If a Premium Seeker views the 3D map of a churned owner and spends a Connect to inquire, the owner receives an automated email: *"A Premium Seeker wants this property based on the 3D tour. Resubscribe to accept the handshake."* This turns historical 3D maps into an eternal lead-generation trap that lures canceled owners back.

## PART 6 — PLATFORM FEATURES (CURRENT + FUTURE)

### The Connects Token Economy (Future)

**Connects** is ScoutIt's internal micro-transaction currency. It powers the entire platform economy.

| Tier | Monthly Connects |
|---|---|
| Free | 1 Connect / month |
| Subscription Tier 1 | +5 Connects |
| Subscription Tier 2 | +10 Connects |
| Earned via Bounty Hunts | Variable |

Connects are spent on:
- Unlocking premium property intel
- Accessing broker contact details
- Unlocking 3D tour previews
- Engaging with the Double-Blind Privacy Shield

### Bounty Hunts (Future)

Crowdsourced data ingestion. Users complete real-world tasks to earn Connects:
- Verify a property address
- Photograph a space
- Confirm a business is still open
- Validate floor area

This creates a **living, self-updating dataset** — the city as a continuously refreshed data source, maintained by its own users. The platform pays out in Connects; users earn access while making the map more accurate.

Bounty Hunts eventually feed into **Luma AI** — users who photograph spaces enable neural 3D reconstruction.

### Luma AI 3D Tours (Future)

Smartphone capture → Luma AI cloud neural rendering → embeddable WebGL 3D tour.

How it gets populated:
- Property owners submit via the submission wizard
- Verified photographers complete Bounty Hunt tasks
- Eventually: Luma's own scanning partners

Unlocked by:
- Subscription tiers
- Spending Connects
- Completing Bounty Hunts that produce the scan

### The Double-Blind Privacy Shield (Future)

Buyers can contact brokers without revealing who they are until *they* choose to introduce themselves.

- Buyer submits inquiry anonymously through ScoutIt
- Broker receives a vetted, anonymous lead
- Buyer controls when and whether to de-anonymize
- ScoutIt acts as the trusted intermediary

This is a direct response to the market's biggest pain point: inquiry = harassment.

### Natural / Enhanced Photo Toggle (Future)

On property detail pages, users can toggle between:
- **Natural**: raw, unprocessed photography
- **Enhanced**: AI-processed lighting, color correction, staging suggestions

Aimed at high-net-worth buyers who distrust overly staged photos but still want to see best-case presentation.

## PART 7 — VISUAL DNA (THE DESIGN SYSTEM)

### The One Rule
> **~95% darkness. ~5% gold.**
> Gold should feel *rare* — like a signature, not wallpaper.

This single ratio is what makes ScoutIt read as luxury. Break it and you break the brand.

### Color Tokens

Always use CSS variables. Never raw hex values.

| Token | Value | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `--bg` | `#0e0e0e` | Page canvas (near-black) |
| `--surface` | `#161616` | Base raised panels |
| `--surface2` | `#1e1e1e` | Elevated cards |
| `--surface3` | `#242424` | Highest surface level |
| `--text-primary` | `#f0ede8` | Headlines (warm off-white) |
| `--text-secondary` | `#c8c8c8` | Body copy |
| `--text-muted` | `rgba(240,237,232,.45)` | Captions, hints |
| `--accent` | `#E8AE3C` | The gold. Signature accent. (refined 2026-06-26 — warmer amber, less yellow than the old `#ffb800`) |
| `--accent-bright` | `#F7C64E` | Interactive gold (buttons, CTAs, hover) |
| `--accent-muted` | `#6E531A` | Subdued gold (borders, dividers) |
| `--border` | `rgba(255,255,255,.07)` | Hairline dividers |
| `--shadow-glow` | `0 0 18px rgba(232,174,60,.45)` | Gold halo for key CTAs (rgb tuple `232, 174, 60`) |
| `--green` `--yellow` `--red` | `#4caf7d` `#e8c84a` `#e8644a` | Semantic states |
| `--brand` | `#1a1814` | Deep brand surfaces |

### Typography

| Role | Font | Style |
|---|---|---|
| `--font-display` | Georgia, serif | Big editorial headlines. E.g., "The Board" |
| `--font-body` | Geist Sans (next/font) | All UI copy, body text |
| `--font-mono` | Geist Mono (next/font) | Labels, eyebrows, data values |

**Eyebrow/label convention:** mono · uppercase · wide letter-spacing (0.3–0.45em) · gold-tinted.  
**Headlines:** serif · large · near-white.

### Motion Rules

- Luxury moves **slow and intentional**. Never snappy.
- `--transition`: 0.38s cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)
- `--transition-fast`: 0.25s
- `--transition-slow`: 0.6s
- Glow is **localized** — the element glows, not the entire screen.

### Signature Visual Elements

**The Cosmic Hero Canvas** — `<canvas>` element, not a photo or video. ~150 twinkling stars, glowing bodies spiraling inward, dust, comets with gold tails, breathing rings, a black-hole darkness pulse, and a gold core. This is the homepage hero.

**Film Grain** — a fixed `.grain` overlay at `z-index: 9999`, opacity ~0.03. Applied globally. Gives every page a filmic, print-quality texture.

**The UFO** — a clickable SVG saucer with green portholes. On click, fires a gold tractor beam onto the ScoutIT wordmark (white "Scout" + gold "IT"). Triggers the scrollytelling manifesto.

### What Is Banned

- Raw hex colors anywhere in component code (use tokens)
- `scroll-snap-type: y mandatory` + `scroll-behavior: smooth` together (causes 30s+ rendering freeze — use `y proximity`)
- Glow "everywhere" — localized bloom only
- Inventing centerpiece visuals — all signature imagery comes from the owner's reference photos in `reference/`

> **Allowed (corrected 2026-06-26 — running code wins over the old "banned" line):**
> - **Tailwind** is used across the app (dashboards especially) alongside vanilla CSS + CSS Modules.
>   Public/marketing surfaces stay vanilla CSS; dashboards may use Tailwind.
> - **Three.js / WebGL** (and a 2D canvas) power the homepage + layer-page backgrounds — permitted
>   because **Lite Mode** (`src/lib/liteMode.js`) disables animations/canvas/WebGL on low-end phones.
> - **Light mode** is not the default but exists: dark is default; light/high-contrast modes live in
>   the Display panel. (Dark stays the brand's signature.)

## PART 8 — DATA ARCHITECTURE (DUAL CMS)

ScoutIt runs two databases simultaneously. Understanding which is which is critical.

### Airtable — The Public Read-Only CMS

Everything the public sees comes from Airtable. It is the editorial CMS.

**Gate:** every record must have its approval checkbox set to `true` before the API serves it.

| Table | Approval field | Key fields |
|---|---|---|
| `PROPERTIES_CMS` | `Approved_For_ScoutIt` | Slug, Title, City, Lat/Lng, SpaceCategory, Photos, BestFor |
| `BROKERS_CMS` | `Approved_For_Live_Site` | BrokerID, Name, SubscriptionLabel, Bio, Image |
| `INTEL_CMS` | `Approved_For_Live_Site` | Slug, Title, Excerpt, BodyParagraph1/2 |

All Airtable data flows through one API proxy: `src/app/api/cms/route.js`. This route also runs Mapbox geocoding (if Lat/Lng is missing) and applies the Haversine radius filter.

**Locally with no API key:** site falls back to mock data in `src/data/`. This is expected — not a bug.

### Supabase — The Private Auth & Submissions DB

Authentication, user state, and owner submissions go here. The public never sees this directly.

> **⚠️ Reconciled 2026-06-24 — the live schema below replaces an earlier (stale) model.**
> The old docs named tables `profiles`, `user_reactions`, `property_submissions`. Those are **gone**.
> Per the 2026-06-22 units handoff: *"Do NOT reintroduce `property_submissions`. The table is `properties`."*
> The running code is the source of truth.

| Table (live) | Purpose |
|---|---|
| `properties` | Owner listings + drafts. Key fields: `owner_id`, `title`, `type`, `space_category`, `slug`, `location`, `price`, `pipeline_status`, `details` (jsonb — incl. `units_inventory`), `coordinates` (PostGIS). **This is the table — not `property_submissions`.** |
| `user_profiles` | User identity/profile. `id` (text key, becomes the Auth UUID-as-text), `display_name`, `subscription_tier`, `connects_balance`, `active_roles`, `badges` (jsonb), etc. |
| `saved_intel` | The buyer's saved/shortlisted properties (the server mirror of the on-device Ledger). `user_id`, `property_id`. |
| `deals` | Broker/owner handshake pitches. `broker_id`, `property_id`, `status`, `pitch_message`. |
| `privacy_settings`, `broker_profiles`, `researcher_profiles` | Per-role extensions of `user_profiles`. |
| `connect_balances`, `connect_transactions` | Connects ledger — **writes only via Edge Functions (service role)**, never client-side. |

**The golden rule:** Airtable = public display. Supabase = private user state. Never mix them.
**Identity note:** real Supabase Auth is the next phase — see `04_DATA_AND_SCHEMA/SUPABASE_AUTH_INTEGRATION_PLAN.md`.

### The Admin Pipeline

Owner submits via the Live Editor / wizard → data lands in Supabase **`properties`** (`pipeline_status = 'pending'`) → admin reviews (`/api/admin/approve`) → if approved, the record is copied to Airtable `PROPERTIES_CMS` (units ride along as `Units_JSON`) → `Approved_For_ScoutIt = true` → property goes live.

## PART 13 — RULES FOR ANYONE WORKING ON SCOUTIT

These apply to every developer, designer, or AI agent:

1. **Read AGENTS.md first.** It is the master prompt. Every rule flows from it.
2. **This is a modified Next.js 16.2.7.** Before writing framework code, check `node_modules/next/dist/docs/`.
3. **Use CSS variables, never raw hex.** `var(--accent)` not `#ffb800`.
4. **95% dark, 5% gold.** Count the gold before you add more.
5. **Never invent the centerpiece visual.** Signature images come from the owner's reference photos in `reference/` (git-ignored, never deployed).
6. **The scroll-snap rule:** use `y proximity` only. Never `mandatory` + `scroll-behavior: smooth` together.
7. **Never push to Vercel / `main` without asking the owner first.** The owner is non-technical. Explain in plain language. `main` must always be deployable.
8. **Verify every route returns 200** after changes.
9. **Build on branches. Commit at clean checkpoints.**
10. **Airtable = public display. Supabase = private state. Never mix.**
